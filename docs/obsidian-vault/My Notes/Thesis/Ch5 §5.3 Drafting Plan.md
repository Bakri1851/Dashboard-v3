### §5.3.1 Performance and Refresh Behaviour (~325 words)

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.3.1 prose block` marker. No longer deferred — numbers lifted from a representative-session prewarm log captured 2026-05-26 evening.

```latex
NFR1 of Chapter~\ref{sec:requirements} requires the V2 stack to be responsive enough for live use within a lab session. The deployed system meets this through the lifespan-prewarm strategy of Chapter~\ref{sec:implementation}, which pre-computes all derived signals before the FastAPI process accepts requests so that the first user request sees the steady-state per-request cost rather than the cold-start cost. The prewarm decomposes into six sequential phases, captured against a representative session of $42{,}443$ submission records spanning $3{,}935$ unique feedback strings.

The submission log loads and parses in $8.0$~s. The OpenAI incorrectness scoring pass runs in $464.5$~s on a cold start with an empty cache, batching $197$ calls for the $3{,}935$ unique feedback strings, but reduces to a sub-second lookup on subsequent runs through the disk-persisted cache at \texttt{data/incorrectness\_cache.json}; this $71\%$ of the cold-start cost only fires once per feedback corpus and never appears in the steady-state cycle. The BKT parameter fit across the six skills present in the cohort runs in $23.2$~s, fitting three skills and falling back to \texttt{config.py} defaults on three under the gating logic of Section~\ref{sec: bkt}. The IRT 2PL fit completes in $7.3$~s on the final $127 \times 86$ response matrix produced by the iterative min-attempts and separable-response drop-pass of Section~\ref{sec: irt}. The struggle leaderboard becomes ready $6.2$~s after the IRT fit and the difficulty leaderboard $3.6$~s after that. The RAG collection takes $137.2$~s to load the sentence-transformers \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} model and embed $3{,}069$ sampled rows into the in-memory vector index.

Total prewarm against this representative session is $649.9$~s on the cold start and approximately $185$~s on a warm start once the incorrectness cache is populated; the RAG embedding pass dominates the warm-start budget. Once the prewarm completes, all subsequent requests respond from in-memory state and the steady-state five-second polling cycle of the lab-assistant SPA was confirmed to refresh without observable latency on the deployment cohort. The full NFR1 verification row appears in Appendix~\ref{app:detailed-test-results}.
```

### §5.3.2 Usability and Interpretability (~230 words)

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.3.2 prose block` marker (line 46).

```latex
NFR2 of Chapter~\ref{sec:requirements} requires the dashboard's metrics and band classifications to be interpretable to a non-specialist instructor without specialist statistical training. The interpretation question was assessed through the survey of Section~\ref{sec:eval-survey}, which probed both the clarity of the underlying struggle and difficulty scores and the intuitiveness of the colour-coded band labels surfaced in the leaderboards. On the five-point clarity scale (Q5 of the instrument), seven of the ten respondents rated each metric as ``Completely Intuitive'' and a further two rated each as ``Slightly Intuitive'', with a single respondent placing both metrics in the ``Slightly Unintuitive'' band; no respondent picked either ``Neutral'' or ``Completely Unintuitive'' on either sub-row, yielding a strongly bimodal distribution where the disagreement collapses to a single outlier. On the five-point intuitiveness scale for the colour-coded bands (Q6), the same bimodal pattern held with six respondents at ``Completely Intuitive'' and three at ``Slightly Intuitive'' on both band sub-rows, with the same single outlier at ``Slightly Unintuitive''. The trust question (Q7) returned seven respondents at ``Very Likely'' or ``Somewhat Likely'' to trust the dashboard's prioritisation (two at ``Very Likely'' and five at ``Somewhat Likely''), against two sceptics at ``Somewhat Unlikely'' or ``Very Unlikely'' and one neutral; the resulting $70/10/20$ favourable--neutral--sceptic split is unpacked in Section~\ref{sec:eval-survey} and revisited as a methodological consideration in Section~\ref{sec:eval-discussion}. The free-text barriers reported under Q4 (nine responses) converge on three themes (staff shortage, visibility of quieter students, and help-seeking reluctance) which the dashboard's prioritisation features directly target. The headline finding is that NFR2 is met for the surveyed cohort within the limitations of the sample size; the full NFR2 verification row appears in Appendix~\ref{app:detailed-test-results}.
```

### §5.3.3 Robustness and Failure Handling (~320 words)

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.3.3 prose block` marker (line 49).

```latex
NFR3 of Chapter~\ref{sec:requirements} requires the dashboard to degrade gracefully rather than fail outright when upstream signals are missing or under-specified. Several edge cases were exercised against the V2 deployment. The cold-start case (no submissions yet at session start) was tested by joining the dashboard before any student had submitted, confirming that the leaderboards render with empty placeholders and the scoring pipeline returns gracefully without raising exceptions; the IRT fit refuses to fit when fewer than two attempts per question are available and falls back to the pre-fit defaults of Section~\ref{sec: irt}. The single-student case (one student active in the session) was tested by replaying a session at the first cutoff; the collaborative-filtering layer requires at least $k+1$ active students to compute a similarity ranking and gracefully returns the parametric ranking unchanged when the cohort is too small. The BKT identifiability case (a cohort where all attempts on a skill share the same outcome, leading to MLE degeneracy) is handled by the gating logic of Section~\ref{sec: bkt}: when fewer than fifty attempts are available or when all attempts are identical, the fitter refuses to fit and the configured defaults are retained; this was exercised by truncating a session to its first ten minutes. The improved-struggle composite's weight-redistribution behaviour (when BKT mastery or IRT difficulty is unavailable for a student) was exercised through the four cases of Section~\ref{sec: improved struggle} and confirmed to return the documented reweighted scores. The lab-state file at \texttt{data/lab\_session.json} was exposed to three-process concurrent writes through the \texttt{filelock} library and survived without data loss across one hundred test cycles. The RAG layer's \texttt{asyncio.to\_thread} offload was confirmed to keep the FastAPI event loop responsive under concurrent RAG requests, with no thread-blocking observed. NFR4 (scalability beyond the deployment cohort of approximately 140 students across twenty-one healthy sessions), NFR5 (privacy invariants under a read-only data flow with no persisted PII), and NFR6 (extensibility through the model toggles of Section~\ref{sec:v2-training}) hold by construction rather than by edge-case test and are documented in the NFR table of Appendix~\ref{app:detailed-test-results}.
```
