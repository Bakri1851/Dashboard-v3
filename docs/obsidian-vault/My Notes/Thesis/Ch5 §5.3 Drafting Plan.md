### §5.3.2 Usability and Interpretability (~230 words)

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.3.2 prose block` marker (line 46).

```latex
NFR2 of Chapter~\ref{sec:design} requires the dashboard's metrics and band classifications to be interpretable to a non-specialist instructor without specialist statistical training. The interpretation question was assessed through the survey of Section~\ref{sec:eval-survey}, which probed both the clarity of the underlying struggle and difficulty scores and the intuitiveness of the colour-coded band labels surfaced in the leaderboards. On the five-point clarity scale (Q5 of the instrument), the eight respondents rated both the student struggle score and the question difficulty score in the upper half of the scale, with no respondent placing either metric in the ``Completely Unintuitive'' or ``Slightly Unintuitive'' bands. On the five-point intuitiveness scale for the colour-coded bands (Q6), the same pattern held for both the struggle and difficulty band labels. The trust question (Q7) returned six respondents at ``Very Likely'' or ``Somewhat Likely'' to trust the dashboard's prioritisation against two sceptics; the $75/25$ net-positive-with-non-trivial-dissent split is unpacked in Section~\ref{sec:eval-survey} and revisited as a methodological consideration in Section~\ref{sec:eval-discussion}. The free-text barriers reported under Q4 converge on three themes (staff shortage, visibility of quieter students, help-seeking reluctance) which the dashboard's prioritisation features directly target. The headline finding is that NFR2 is met for the surveyed cohort within the limitations of the sample size; the full NFR2 verification row appears in Appendix~\ref{app:detailed-test-results}.
```

### §5.3.3 Robustness and Failure Handling (~320 words)

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.3.3 prose block` marker (line 49).

```latex
NFR3 of Chapter~\ref{sec:design} requires the dashboard to degrade gracefully rather than fail outright when upstream signals are missing or under-specified. Several edge cases were exercised against the V2 deployment. The cold-start case (no submissions yet at session start) was tested by joining the dashboard before any student had submitted, confirming that the leaderboards render with empty placeholders and the scoring pipeline returns gracefully without raising exceptions; the IRT fit refuses to fit when fewer than two attempts per question are available and falls back to the pre-fit defaults of Section~\ref{sec: irt}. The single-student case (one student active in the session) was tested by replaying a session at the first cutoff; the collaborative-filtering layer requires at least $k+1$ active students to compute a similarity ranking and gracefully returns the parametric ranking unchanged when the cohort is too small. The BKT identifiability case (a cohort where all attempts on a skill share the same outcome, leading to MLE degeneracy) is handled by the gating logic of Section~\ref{sec: bkt}: when fewer than fifty attempts are available or when all attempts are identical, the fitter refuses to fit and the configured defaults are retained; this was exercised by truncating a session to its first ten minutes. The improved-struggle composite's weight-redistribution behaviour (when BKT mastery or IRT difficulty is unavailable for a student) was exercised through the four cases of Section~\ref{sec: improved struggle} and confirmed to return the documented reweighted scores. The lab-state file at \texttt{data/lab\_session.json} was exposed to three-process concurrent writes through the \texttt{filelock} library and survived without data loss across one hundred test cycles. The RAG layer's \texttt{asyncio.to\_thread} offload was confirmed to keep the FastAPI event loop responsive under concurrent RAG requests, with no thread-blocking observed. NFR4 (scalability beyond the deployment cohort of approximately sixty students per session), NFR5 (privacy invariants under a read-only data flow with no persisted PII), and NFR6 (extensibility through the model toggles of Section~\ref{sec:v2-training}) hold by construction rather than by edge-case test and are documented in the NFR table of Appendix~\ref{app:detailed-test-results}.
```
