## §5.4.3 Hyperparameter optimisation (~290 words)

**Anchor**: `% TODO: 5.4.3 prose block` at [`results-and-evaluation.tex:231`](../../../../Report/main-sections/results-and-evaluation.tex#L231), under `\label{sec:eval-hyperopt}`. Paste the prose immediately below the TODO comment and immediately above the existing `\begin{figure}` blocks for `fig:eval-hyperparams-contour` and `fig:eval-hyperparams-importances`. The two figures are the joint-study pair (contour + parameter-importance bar chart); the table `tab:eval-hyperparams` is already wrapped in `\resizebox{\textwidth}{!}{...}` so it fits the page.

```latex
Two scalar hyperparameters were tuned using Optuna's Tree-structured Parzen Estimator \cite{NIPS2011_86e8f7ab, akibaOptunaNextgenerationHyperparameter2019}, maximising session-grouped five-fold cross-validation Spearman~$\rho$ against the LLM's four-band rating. The Bayesian shrinkage constant $K$ was tuned in a 50-trial study over integers in $[1, 20]$, and the collaborative-filtering similarity threshold $\tau$ in a separate 50-trial study over reals in $[0.4, 0.9]$. TPE was preferred over grid or random search as the two search spaces are small enough for Bayesian methods to converge in tens of trials where a uniform sweep would require an order of magnitude more. The shrinkage constant $K$ moves from v1 default $5$ to v2 best $1$, with CV $\rho$ changing from $+0.471$ to $+0.481$ --- $\Delta\rho \approx +0.013$, within the per-fold standard deviation, so the result is a robustness validation of the hand-set value rather than an empirical refinement. The threshold $\tau$ moves from v1 default $0.7$ to v2 best $0.900$, with CV $\rho$ moving from $+0.306$ to $+0.466$ --- $\Delta\rho \approx +0.200$, a substantial improvement indicating that the v1 default was too permissive. The best $\tau$ sits at the upper boundary of the $[0.4, 0.9]$ search range, so a finer search may yield further improvement; this is noted as future work in Section~\ref{sec:eval-discussion}.

To validate the independence assumption that justified the two separate studies, a single joint $K \times \tau$ study was run over 100 trials with the joint objective $0.5\,(\rho_K + \rho_\tau)$. The joint optimum lands at $K = 1$ and $\tau = 0.898$, coinciding with the independent optima to within the third decimal place; this confirms that the two hyperparameters are effectively decoupled under the present objective. Figure~\ref{fig:eval-hyperparams-contour} shows the joint optimisation surface as a contour plot, with the high-$\rho$ ridge tracking $\tau$ near the upper boundary and the surface essentially flat along the $K$ axis. Figure~\ref{fig:eval-hyperparams-importances} quantifies this directly as a parameter-importance bar chart: $\tau$ accounts for the bulk of the joint-objective variance whereas $K$ contributes negligibly. The four BKT priors and the BKT mastery threshold were originally on the grid but were dropped from the v2 study as each trial would have required around thirty minutes of BKT refits; they remain at \texttt{config.py} defaults. Table~\ref{tab:eval-hyperparams} reports the headline numbers.
```

## §5.4.4 Findings (~900 words)

**Anchor**: `% TODO: 5.4.4 prose block` at [`results-and-evaluation.tex:278`](../../../../Report/main-sections/results-and-evaluation.tex#L278), under `\label{sec:eval-findings}`. Paste the prose immediately below the TODO comment and immediately above the existing `\begin{figure}` blocks for `fig:eval-weights-struggle`, `fig:eval-weights-heatmap`, `fig:eval-ols-diagnostic`, `fig:eval-pred-vs-obs-struggle`, `fig:eval-rho-perfold-struggle`, and `fig:eval-negative-findings`. This subsubsection collects all three v2 weight-refit findings as one continuous narrative: the headline positive struggle finding, the two weaker positive findings (difficulty and improved model), and the verdict scorecard table that consolidates the per-component winners across both this section and Section~\ref{sec:eval-hyperopt}. The narrative arc is positive headline → negative findings → consolidated verdict; the section closes with a two-regime deployment recommendation.

```latex
The v2 refit programme produced three trained weight vectors, scored under the protocol of Section~\ref{sec:eval-v2-methodology} against the LLM rater's four-band severity rating. The headline finding is the seven-signal struggle model; the difficulty refit yields a moderate positive correlation; the improved-struggle refit yields a weaker positive finding that nonetheless matches the achievable ceiling under a non-linear model class; the verdict for each weight vector and for the two hyperparameters of Section~\ref{sec:eval-hyperopt} is consolidated in Table~\ref{tab:eval-verdict-scorecard} as four positive findings and one tie.

\paragraph{Struggle model (v1 vs v2).}
The seven-signal baseline struggle model was re-fitted as an ordinary least-squares linear regression against the LLM rater's four-band severity rating, using session-grouped five-fold splits so that no student appears in both train and test of a given fold. The seven raw signals ($\hat{n}, \hat{t}, \hat{\imath}, \hat{r}, \widehat{A^{\mathrm{raw}}}, \hat{d}, \widehat{\mathrm{rep}}$) enter as features after the same per-(session, cutoff) min-max normalisation the live model uses. The mean held-out Spearman $\rho$ is $+0.588$ with a 95\% interval $[+0.490,\, +0.686]$ across the five folds, against a v1 baseline of $\rho = +0.471$ on the same labels; the per-fold values are stable in $[+0.498,\, +0.666]$.

The substantive empirical finding is two weight sign-flips, made visible at a glance in Figure~\ref{fig:eval-weights-struggle}. Active time $\hat{t}$ moves from v1 $+0.100$ to v2 $-0.214$, so v2 treats high raw activity as anti-correlated with severity --- engaged students stay logged in for longer. Answer-repetition $\widehat{\mathrm{rep}}$ moves from $+0.070$ to $-0.126$, with the same direction; the rater's notion of struggle aligns more with abandonment than with repetition. Submission count $\hat{n}$ remains positive but shrinks from $+0.100$ to $+0.042$, so the v2 weight is a smaller positive contribution rather than the flipped sign returned by the gpt-4o-mini era refit. Mean incorrectness $\hat{\imath}$ moves from $+0.200$ to $+0.103$, while the recent-incorrectness EWMA $\widehat{A^{\mathrm{raw}}}$ is trimmed from $+0.380$ to $+0.314$ but remains the largest contributor. Figure~\ref{fig:eval-rho-perfold-struggle} shows the per-fold Spearman ρ stability that underwrites the headline interval, and Figure~\ref{fig:eval-ols-diagnostic} plots the predicted band severity from the OLS output against the observed LLM band as a binned-mean calibration curve. Table~\ref{tab:eval-weights-struggle} lists the seven weights with per-fold standard deviations and sign-flip flags.

\paragraph{Difficulty model (moderate positive correlation).}
The five-signal baseline difficulty model was re-fitted under the same OLS protocol with leave-one-out cross-validation on the seventy-two questions. The pooled Spearman $\rho$ is $+0.468$ --- a moderate positive rank correlation against the LLM's four-band difficulty rating, against a near-flat v1 baseline on the same labels. The improvement is the largest absolute $\Delta$ on the refit programme; under the upgraded gpt-4o rater the cohort-skew limitation (76\% of the seventy-two questions are rated Very Hard) is no longer the dominant ceiling on the difficulty model that it was under the gpt-4o-mini era. Only one of the five weights flips sign in v2: the mean response time $\tilde{t}$ moves from $+0.120$ to $-0.146$, so a longer mean response time on a question signals lower difficulty under the v2 weighting --- consistent with engaged students dwelling on questions they are working through. The column-normalised count $\tilde{c}$ stays positive but drops from $+0.280$ to $+0.174$, mean attempts $\tilde{a}$ stays positive at $+0.227$, the failure-rate $\tilde{f}$ trims from $+0.200$ to $+0.096$, and the partial-credit term $\tilde{p}$ grows from $+0.200$ to $+0.356$ to become the largest contributor. Table~\ref{tab:eval-weights-difficulty} lists the five weights; the two-panel comparison in Figure~\ref{fig:eval-negative-findings} pairs these with the improved-struggle weights below.

\paragraph{Improved-struggle model (weaker positive, with caveat).}
The three-weight improved-struggle model was re-fitted under the same OLS protocol, taking the behavioural composite $B_s$, the mastery-gap term $M_s$, and the difficulty-adjusted exposure term $D_s$ as features and the LLM's four-band severity rating as target. The mean held-out Spearman $\rho$ is $+0.201$ with interval $[+0.047,\, +0.356]$, against a near-flat v1 baseline on the same labels. A separate model-class bake-off run against the same labels found that a non-linear RandomForest fit reaches $\rho \approx +0.202$ on this composite, so the linear OLS fit reported here sits at the achievable ceiling under the upgraded rater rather than under-fitting the data. The trained weights are $(w_B, w_M, w_D) = (+0.268, -0.680, -0.052)$; OLS assigned negative weight to the BKT mastery-gap term and to the IRT-adjusted exposure term, indicating that, conditional on $B_s$ being in the model, both upstream signals are anti-correlated with the rater's severity rating rather than independently informative. Two caveats sit alongside this finding. First, the v1 design hypothesis --- that BKT mastery-gap and IRT-adjusted exposure contribute orthogonal information to a convex combination --- is not corroborated on this cohort. Second, the improved-struggle model as a whole still ranks worse than the simpler seven-signal struggle model ($\rho = +0.201$ against $+0.588$); the trained weights are an improvement over the v1 weights for this model, but the deployed dashboard default should stay on the seven-signal struggle model rather than switch to improved-struggle. Table~\ref{tab:eval-weights-improved} lists the three weights.

\paragraph{Verdict and deployment recommendation.}
Table~\ref{tab:eval-verdict-scorecard} consolidates the per-component verdicts across the v2 refit programme: v2 wins on the struggle model, the difficulty model, the improved-struggle model, and the CF threshold $\tau$; the shrinkage constant $K$ is tied within fold-variance noise. The verdict is four positive findings and one tie. The practical deployment recommendation is to switch to v2 weights for the struggle model and to v2's $\tau = 0.900$; the v2 difficulty refit lifts difficulty rank quality from a near-flat v1 baseline to a moderate positive correlation, and the improved-struggle refit is positive but still under-ranks the simpler seven-signal struggle model; the shrinkage constant $K$ toggle is a no-op. The band-classification disagreement induced by switching to v2 struggle weights is examined in Section~\ref{sec:eval-discussion}.
```
