## §5.6.2 Tradeoffs and Threats to Validity (~510 words)

> **Sync note 2026-05-26 (ToC trim)**: the deleted §5.4.8 Observed Strengths and Weaknesses block was synthesised into the first `\paragraph{}` of this prose, so the surviving content lands in §5.6 rather than being lost.

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.6.2 prose block` marker in [`results-and-evaluation.tex`](../../../../Report/main-sections/results-and-evaluation.tex).

```latex
The chapter's evaluation rests on several methodological tradeoffs that should be made explicit before the limitations of the deployed system are discussed. Before reviewing them it is useful to summarise the empirical strengths and weaknesses of the v2 refit programme that the tradeoffs qualify.

\paragraph{Observed strengths and weaknesses.}
The seven-signal baseline struggle model, both under v1 hand-set weights ($\rho = +0.423$) and under v2 OLS-trained weights ($\rho = +0.573$), is the most informative single ranking surface the dashboard exposes; the v2 refit produced three substantive weight sign-flips that are interpretable through the deployment cohort's specific characteristics, namely that engaged students submit more and that repeating answers signals less severity than abandonment. The collaborative-filtering threshold $\tau$ was the largest substantive refinement under the hyperparameter optimisation, lifting CF rank quality from $\rho = +0.234$ at $\tau = 0.7$ to $\rho = +0.434$ at $\tau = 0.899$, and the joint $K \times \tau$ surface of Section~\ref{sec:eval-hyperopt} additionally confirmed that the two hyperparameters are effectively decoupled. The five-signal baseline difficulty model is the weakest ranking surface at $\rho = +0.287$, limited by a cohort where $76\%$ of questions sit in the Very Hard band and there is little rank resolution to recover. The three-weight improved-struggle blend, even with v2-trained weights, ranks substantially below the seven-signal baseline struggle model alone ($\rho = +0.168$ vs $+0.573$); the v1 design hypothesis that BKT mastery-gap and IRT-adjusted exposure add orthogonal information was not corroborated on this cohort.

\paragraph{Rater fidelity.}
The v2 weight refits in Section~\ref{sec:eval-findings} use the LLM rater's four-band severity rating as ground truth, but the inter-rater agreement check of Section~\ref{sec:eval-compare-struggle} returns Cohen's $\kappa$ values that fall in the ``slight'' band of the Landis--Koch scale. The v2 rank-quality numbers therefore measure rank agreement with the LLM rater, not rank agreement with a human-authoritative ground truth. The within-one-band agreement of $70\%$ is the silver lining --- raters cluster around adjacent bands rather than opposite extremes --- but a fully validated ground truth would require an instructor-flagging study that the deployment scope did not permit.

\paragraph{Sample size and external validity.}
The deployment cohort is a single instructor's module across approximately twenty-one healthy sessions; the rank-quality intervals reported throughout the chapter are narrow within the cohort but say nothing about external generalisation to other cohorts, instructors, or institutions. The deployment is therefore reported as evidence from one site rather than a general claim about the dashboard's performance class.

\paragraph{Model-class constraint.}
The v2 weight refits are constrained to ordinary least-squares linear regression so that the v1 and v2 weight vectors are directly comparable term by term and so that the deployed band thresholds continue to apply unchanged. Richer model classes such as gradient boosting or learned-to-rank approaches might achieve higher absolute Spearman $\rho$ but at the cost of interpretability and of the existing threshold pipeline; the tradeoff was made in favour of interpretability and is documented as a deliberate restriction rather than an oversight.

\paragraph{Hyperparameter scope.}
The Optuna study of Section~\ref{sec:eval-hyperopt} tuned only the Bayesian shrinkage constant $K$ and the collaborative-filtering threshold $\tau$. The four BKT priors and the BKT mastery threshold were pinned at \texttt{config.py} defaults because each Optuna trial would require around thirty minutes of BKT refits; the deferred-parameter set could move the BKT AUC of Section~\ref{sec:eval-findings} but is unlikely to invalidate the headline verdict, and it remains future work.
```

## §5.6.3 Limitations (~430 words)

**Anchor**: existing `% TODO: 5.6.3 prose block` marker.

```latex
The deployed system carries several known limitations that are distinct from the methodological tradeoffs above; these affect the dashboard's operational behaviour rather than the evaluation's interpretive scope.

\paragraph{Sparse-data degeneracy.}
The BKT mastery model requires at least fifty attempts per skill to fit reliably (Section~\ref{sec: bkt}); below that threshold the gating logic refuses to fit and the configured defaults are retained, which means BKT-derived signals are absent on small classes or early in a session. The IRT 2PL fit similarly requires at least two attempts per student and per question (Section~\ref{sec: irt}); rows that fail this filter are dropped before aggregation. The mistake-clustering pipeline requires at least three incorrect submissions per question to produce a cluster (Section~\ref{sec: mistake clustering}). These thresholds are not severe in the deployment cohort but would constrain the dashboard's usefulness on smaller or earlier sessions.

\paragraph{Collaborative-filtering cold start.}
The collaborative-filtering layer needs at least $k+1$ active students in the cohort to compute a similarity neighbourhood; on a session with fewer active students the CF ranking falls back to the parametric ranking unchanged. This is a graceful degradation rather than a failure, but it means CF's elevation behaviour is unavailable at the start of a session before enough students have submitted.

\paragraph{Smart-device integration.}
FR6 of Chapter~\ref{sec:design} (native smart-device integration beyond the responsive lab-assistant single-page application) was scoped out at design time and remains unimplemented. The deployed assistant SPA is responsive on phones but does not push notifications, leverage smartwatch displays, or surface a separate compact-glance view; these are documented as future work.

\paragraph{Privacy and student comfort.}
The survey of Section~\ref{sec:eval-survey} returned a $25\%$ minority of respondents who reported feeling ``Somewhat Uncomfortable'' with being scored under a per-student struggle score, and the free-text responses to Q8 and Q10 raised concerns about visibility of their difficulties to instructors and about persistence of the data beyond the session. The dashboard's existing privacy invariants (read-only API, anonymised identifiers, no personal data persisted, RAG retrieval scoped per session) address the most-cited concerns, but the comfort dimension is not fully resolved by technical privacy guarantees alone; this is flagged for future ethical-review discussion.

\paragraph{Reproducibility of the v2 refit.}
The v2 refit programme depends on the GPT-4o-mini rater, which is an external commercial service. If the model is deprecated, retrained, or has its pricing structure changed, the v2 weight refits would need to be re-run against the replacement rater or against a different second-opinion strategy. The pipeline of Section~\ref{sec:v2-pipeline-design} is reproducible in structure but not in the LLM artefact itself, and the v2 weights are tied to a specific rater snapshot.
```
