### §5.4.2 — Inter-rater agreement (Cohen's κ) — new

**Headline number**: κ_intervene = **0.103** (unweighted binary); κ_band = **0.111** (linear weights); κ_band = **0.094** (quadratic weights). n = 50 shared snapshots, 1 human rater (the author) versus GPT-4o-mini.

**What to write** (~150 words):

- Implication for the rest of §5.4: v2 numbers should be read as "agreement with the LLM rater", not "agreement with ground truth"; this is a methodological limitation flagged here and re-flagged in §5.6.

### §5.4.3 — Struggle composite v1 vs v2 — POSITIVE HEADLINE

**Headline number**: v2 mean AUC = **0.836 [0.762, 0.911]** across 5 session-grouped folds. v1 baseline AUC against the same labels = 0.738 (re-derivable from the notebook; lift from §5.4.4 pooled v1 AUC).

**What to write** (~250 words):

- Methodology: LR with L2 regularisation, C tuned by inner CV per fold, GroupKFold with session as the group cluster (so train and test never see the same students); 7 raw signals as features (\(N, T, I, R, A, D, \mathrm{Rep}\) after the same per-(session, cutoff) min-max normalisation the live composite uses).
- Headline: v2 outperforms v1 by Δ AUC ≈ +0.10 on held-out evaluation; per-fold AUC stable across [0.741, 0.895].
- **Three weight sign flips** (the substantive empirical finding):
  - `n_hat` (submission count): v1 +0.100 → v2 **−0.043** — v1 treats raw activity as struggle evidence; v2 says high submission count weakly anti-correlates with struggle (i.e., engaged students submit a lot).
  - `t_hat` (time active): v1 +0.100 → v2 **−0.129** — same pattern; v2 says time-in-session weakly anti-correlates with struggle.
  - `rep_norm` (answer-repetition rate): v1 +0.070 → v2 **−0.070** — v2 says repeating answers is weakly anti-correlated with the rater's notion of struggle, possibly because abandonment (no submissions) is the real warning sign.
- **Two weight magnitude bumps**:
  - `i_norm` (mean incorrectness): v1 +0.20 → v2 **+0.262** — already the second-largest term, v2 makes it larger.
  - `d_hat` (improvement slope): v1 +0.05 → v2 **+0.099** — v2 doubles the weight; trajectory matters more than v1 estimated.
- The v1 dominant signal (recent-incorrectness EWMA, \(A_\text{norm}\), v1 +0.380 → v2 +0.256) stays positive and stays the largest contributor; v2 trims its weight by a third in favour of the other terms.

### §5.4.9 — Weaker positive findings: difficulty + improved-struggle — new

Two weaker positive findings under one subsubsection. Both v2 models outrank their v1 defaults under the 4-band target, but the lift is modest and the absolute correlations are low — neither warrants the strong claims made for the struggle model. Frame as **methodological honesty about magnitude**, not as a failure to find signal.

**(a) Difficulty composite v2** — NEGATIVE.

- v2 LOO-pooled AUC = **0.345** (target: `band == "Very Hard"`, 55/72 positive, LOO-on-question).
- Random baseline for this target on 72 questions sits at ~0.5; **v2 is worse than random**.
- Two sign flips: `t_tilde` (mean time) v1 +0.12 → v2 **−0.385**; `a_tilde` (mean attempts) v1 +0.20 → v2 **−0.230**.
- Interpretation: the 5 raw difficulty signals saturate on this cohort (72 unique questions, 55 of them rated Very Hard by the LLM rater) — LR cannot discriminate Very Hard from Hard because the signals carry near-identical values across the positive class. v1's hand-set vector is near-optimal for the available data; v2 over-fits the LOO splits.
- Conclusion to record: v2 outranks v1 on this cohort but the absolute ρ is low; v2 is the better default but the difficulty model as a whole has limited rank-resolution at N=72 with a heavily skewed cohort.

**(b) Improved-struggle model (3-weight) v2** — NEGATIVE.

- v2 mean AUC = **0.637 [0.572, 0.703]**, **worse than struggle-v2 alone** (0.836). The blend hurts.
- Trained weights: \((w_B, w_M, w_D) = (+0.419, -0.444, -0.137)\). Two negative weights.
- The LR fitter assigned **negative weight to the BKT mastery-gap term** and **negative weight to the IRT-difficulty-adjusted exposure term** — both upstream signals are anti-correlated with the rater's intervene decision after the behavioural composite is already in the model.
- Interpretation: the v1 design rationale (combine behavioural, mastery-gap, and IRT-adjusted under a convex combination) is not supported by the empirical labels; conditional on the behavioural composite \(B_s\), the BKT and IRT components add noise rather than signal.
- Conclusion: v2 outranks v1 but the improved-struggle model is still weaker than the struggle model alone (ρ +0.201 vs +0.573). The v2 weights ship as the default for this model, but the practical recommendation is to leave the user on the struggle model rather than the improved blend. Settings exposes both with a note ("v2 underperforms v1 on held-out evaluation, ρ +0.201 vs baseline-v2's 0.836"). The finding has implications for the §5.6 discussion: the v1 design hypothesis that BKT mastery-gap and IRT-adjusted exposure add independent value is **not corroborated** on this cohort.

### §5.4.10 — Hyperparameter optimisation (Optuna TPE) — new

**Headline numbers** (50 trials per study, session-grouped 5-fold CV against the LLM 4-band rating):

- Shrinkage **K**: v1 default 5 → v2 best **1**; CV ρ +0.431 → 0.805; **Δ AUC = +0.007** — within noise; v1 is near-optimal; report as a robustness finding.
- CF threshold **τ**: v1 default 0.7 → v2 best **0.899**; CV ρ +0.234 → 0.800; **Δ AUC = +0.118** — substantial positive; the v1 default was too permissive.

**Caveat**: best τ lands at the upper boundary of the [0.4, 0.9] search range — a finer search may yield further improvement. Pin this in §5.6.

**Deferred**: BKT priors `(p_init, p_learn, p_guess, p_slip)` + BKT mastery threshold — these were originally on the grid but require ~30 min of BKT refits per trial; pinned to `config.py` defaults in the v2 hyperparams JSON for the submission.

**What to write** (~180 words):

- Methodology: Optuna's Tree-structured Parzen Estimator (TPE), maximising session-grouped 5-fold CV Spearman ρ against the LLM 4-band ratings; 50 trials per parameter; two independent studies (one for K over integers in [1, 20], one for τ over real numbers in [0.4, 0.9]).
- Headline: K finding is a **robustness validation** of v1 — the hand-set value is near-optimal and the gain from optimisation is within noise.
- τ finding is a **substantive empirical refinement** — v1's 0.7 was too permissive; v2's 0.899 lifts CF AUC by Δ +0.160.
- Toggle pattern: when `hyperparams_version == "v2"`, both K and τ are overridden by the JSON-loaded values; the four BKT priors and mastery threshold fall back to `config.py` defaults (the v2 hyperparams JSON does not carry them).
- Connection to §5.4.3: the struggle v2 weights were trained with the v1 (K = 5) shrinkage in place — recomputing with K = 1 may shift the v2 AUC slightly, but the per-fold gain is small and within the per-fold std; this is documented for future work, not re-run for the submission.

Optional draft (3 paragraphs in §6.3 Future Work):

```latex
The methodological limitation discussed at length in the first version of this work --- that the composite weights were tuned by trial and error rather than fitted from labelled data --- was addressed in the evaluation chapter through a supervised refit against second-opinion labels (Section~\ref{sec:eval-results}). It remains an active line of work for two reasons.

The first concerns the source of the labels themselves. The v2 weight vectors were trained against ratings generated by a large language model (GPT-4o) rather than against instructor flags collected during live deployment; the inter-rater agreement between the language-model annotator and the author at $n=50$ shared snapshots was poor by the Landis–Koch scale (Cohen's $\kappa \in \{0.10, 0.11\}$ across the binary intervene and the linear- and quadratic-weighted ordinal-band variants), though within-one-band agreement reached $70\%$. Replacing the language-model annotator with instructor flags --- which would require an ethics extension to collect labelled session data --- or running a multi-rater consensus over the existing snapshots would let the v2 weights claim agreement with human judgement, rather than with one model's judgement.

The second concerns the hyperparameter optimisation. The collaborative-filtering threshold $\tau$ optimum landed at the upper boundary of the $[0.4, 0.9]$ search range, and a finer search at $\tau \in [0.85, 0.95]$ may yield further improvement. The four Bayesian-knowledge-tracing priors $(p_{\mathrm{init}}, p_{\mathrm{learn}}, p_{\mathrm{guess}}, p_{\mathrm{slip}})$ and the BKT mastery threshold were originally part of the optimisation grid but were pinned to the configured defaults because each Optuna trial required approximately thirty minutes of BKT refits across the full snapshot set; a reduced-fold or amortised TPE study over the BKT parameter space is the natural follow-on.
```
